Studio Chronicle Research, robotics & narrative craft

My practice studies the tension between precise engineering and unruly imagination—channeling sketches, motion, and light into devices that feel ceremonial rather than sterile.

From tendon-driven prosthetics at Khalifa University to conversational AI surfaces, I choreograph how technology greets the body. Each artifact is built with empathy labs, field interviews, and cinematic prototypes that invite participation.

Recent residencies span MBZUAI, Philips Middle East, and collaborative workshops across the GCC. My work centers inclusive design, tactile feedback, and the storytelling rituals that make emerging tech feel intimate and human.

Outside the lab, I mentor hackathon teams, teach STEM rituals with care, and keep sprawling sketchbooks of imagined interfaces. The work is equal parts poem and protocol.
